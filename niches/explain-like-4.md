# Explain Everyday Things To A 4-Year-Old

## Working Definition

Age-appropriate scripts and mini-explanations for parents answering everyday questions from a 4-year-old: nature, machines, feelings, rules, bodies, money, death, weather, holidays, social situations, and "why" questions.

## Current Score

69 / 100, low confidence.

## Why It Looks Promising

- Parents regularly need simple language in the moment.
- Evergreen content engine is strong.
- Firsthand parenting perspective can improve tone and practicality.
- Pages can include "say this", "avoid saying this", follow-up questions, and a tiny activity.

## Demand Estimate

Qualitative: medium.

Likely demand clusters:

- "how to explain death to a 4 year old"
- "how to explain money to preschoolers"
- "how to explain electricity to a child"
- "why do toddlers ask why"
- "how to explain sharing to a 4 year old"
- "how to explain where babies come from preschooler"

Confidence: low until SERPs are segmented by safe everyday topics vs sensitive topics.

## Competition Estimate

Qualitative: medium-high.

Expected competitors:

- Verywell Family, Parents.com, child psychologists, children's hospitals, PBS/education sites, parenting blogs, finance sites for money topics.

Potential weak spots:

- Non-sensitive everyday explanations.
- Concrete scripts for ages 3-4 rather than generic "kids."
- Pages combining answer + follow-up activity + parent troubleshooting.

## Safety And Trust Concerns

- Sensitive topics like death, bodies, consent, violence, divorce, illness, religion, disasters, and trauma need care.
- Medical, mental health, and developmental claims should cite authoritative sources or be avoided.
- This may need expert review if it becomes advice-heavy.

## Page Templates

1. Parent script: "How to explain X to a 4-year-old"
2. Everyday why answer: "Why does X happen?"
3. Sensitive-topic guide with conservative boundaries.
4. Conversation starter with follow-up questions.
5. Mini activity to demonstrate the idea.
6. "What not to say" guide.
7. Book pairing guide.
8. Age comparison: 3 vs 4 vs 5.
9. Social rule explainer: sharing, waiting, privacy, kindness.
10. Parent cheat sheet: one-minute answers.

## What Would Make This Continue

- SERPs show weak or generic pages for everyday topics.
- We can focus on low-risk topics first.
- There is a clear content format that is more practical than generic parenting advice.

## What Would Pause Or Kill It

- Most good queries are sensitive YMYL-style topics.
- Results are dominated by major medical/child-development sites.
- The content starts sounding like expert advice rather than parent-tested scripts.
