## Recommended Content Focus

### Priority 1: Finish The Age-4 STEM Cluster

Keep the current priority stack. Test the five activities in `site/collections/original-stem-activities-for-4-year-olds.html`, record observations, and upgrade winners.

Focus upgrades on:

- Photos or simple diagrams.
- Parent-tested setup time.
- What a child actually said or did.
- What failed and how to rescue the activity.
- Easier/harder variants.
- Specific safety and mess boundaries.

This cluster has low measured difficulty and strong differentiation potential.

### Priority 2: Strengthen The Broader Preschool STEM Page

The broader `stem activities for preschoolers` keyword has much higher volume and still low measured KD. Do not make it a generic list. Use it as the hub that routes to:

- Age-4 STEM original pack.
- Science experiments for 4 year olds.
- Engineering/building activities.
- No-prep STEM.
- Individual tested cards.

The page should make Kid Activity Lab's method obvious: quick setup, one kid-facing mission, one test loop, safety boundaries, and real parent notes.

### Priority 3: Expand Into Science Experiments Only After Testing

`science experiments for 4 year olds` has decent volume but higher difficulty. It is worth improving, but only with original evidence. Best subtopics from Semrush:

- easy science experiments for 4 year olds
- at home science experiments for 4 year olds
- simple science experiments for 4 year olds
- science experiments for 4 year olds at home

### Priority 4: Treat Fine Motor As A Later Cluster

Fine motor has the largest volume in the current data, but the SERP is more expert/therapy-adjacent. It can work if the site builds a strong home-practical angle, but it should not distract from the age-4 STEM push yet.

If pursued, use angles like:

- fine motor activities for preschoolers at home
- no-prep fine motor activities
- fine motor activities with household objects
- 5-minute fine motor activities

### Priority 5: Be Selective With Low-Volume Support Pages

Pages like toy car, cardboard, painter tape, no-cut, and building can be useful internal assets. They should not all be indexed until they have enough unique content, photos, parent notes, or material-specific guidance.
