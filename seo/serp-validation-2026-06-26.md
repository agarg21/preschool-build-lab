# SERP Validation: Age 3-6 Activity Pages

Date: 2026-06-26

## Decision

Publish 10 indexable SEO landing pages for parent searches around activities for 3, 4, 5, and 6 year old kids.

The page format should be:

- Search-matched title and H1.
- A quick chooser table.
- 8-9 concrete activity blocks.
- Materials, time, mess, adult-help level, parent safety check.
- Internal links to one-screen cards where available.

This is better than long guides because parents searching these phrases usually need fast usable ideas, not a long article.

## Source Notes

- CDC milestone pages frame ages by how children play, learn, speak, act, and move.
- NAEYC emphasizes play-based, developmentally appropriate learning.
- AAP/HealthyChildren encourages families to preserve space for offline activities such as reading, outdoor play, games, and hobbies.
- Fine motor SERPs include occupational therapy and parent resources, and often mention household-item activities and supervision around small objects.
- STEM SERPs often include broad classroom lists; our wedge is quick at-home engineering prompts.

## Impression Expectations

For a brand-new domain with thin authority, a realistic early expectation is modest:

- Month 1: 0-300 total impressions if indexing is slow.
- Month 2: 300-1,500 total impressions if most pages index and Google tests long-tail queries.
- Better outcome: 2,000+ impressions if age pages start matching multiple variants.

The key metric is not clicks first. It is which queries Google starts showing us for. Use Search Console after 4-8 weeks to decide which clusters deserve more pages.
