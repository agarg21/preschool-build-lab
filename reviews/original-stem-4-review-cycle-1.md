# Original STEM for 4 Year Olds - Review Cycle 1

Date: 2026-06-29

## Overall Verdict

The activities are good enough to keep, but the page reads more like an internal field-test brief than a parent-ready activity page. A tired parent should be able to understand each activity in under 60 seconds, and the current version needs more exact setup positions, safety boundaries, mess limits, and adult/kid separation.

## Page-Level Fixes

1. Add a short quick-start block to each activity: time, adult job, and stop rule.
2. Add one clear safety or mess boundary per activity.
3. Rewrite kid steps as short commands a parent can read aloud.
4. Give each activity a concrete test loop and stop cue.
5. Add a success note so parents know free play or partial redesign still counts.

## Activity Notes

### Ramp Detective

Keep. Add a measuring method, suggested first surfaces, low-ramp safety, and a stop rule after three roads.

### Bridge Rescue

Keep. Use a 4-6 inch starting gap, begin with paper so the bridge can bend productively, and add a line for what to say after failure.

### Shadow Builder

Change. Clarify that the flashlight stays still and the toy moves between flashlight and wall. Add a no-faces flashlight safety note.

### Windproof Tower

Keep. Make the parent the wind for the first test, define "gentle wind" as three slow fan waves, and keep towers below chest height.

### Tiny Boat Cargo Test

Keep with safety edits. Use one inch of water, a towel under the tray, large cargo pieces only, and an adult-made first foil boat.
