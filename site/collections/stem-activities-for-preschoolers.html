STEM

STEM activities for preschoolers.

For preschoolers, STEM can be simple: build something, test it, notice what happened, and change one thing. These activities avoid long explanations and let the object do the teaching.

Quick pick: Use the same prompt on every activity: What do you think will happen if we change one thing?

Car Ramp Distance Test

early STEM, measuring, prediction

4-6 years 5 min low mess medium help
Needramp, tape, toy cars
  1. Roll a car.
  2. Mark where it stops.
  3. Try another car.
  4. Compare the marks.

Parent check: Keep the rolling area clear.

Paper Bridge

engineering, problem solving, testing

4-6 years 3 min low mess medium help
Needpaper, two books, toy car
  1. Put books apart.
  2. Lay paper across.
  3. Try a car.
  4. Fold paper stronger.

Parent check: Keep books low and stable.

Cardboard Car Ramp

quick building, toy car play, cause and effect

3-5 years 2 min low mess low help
Needcardboard, books, toy cars
  1. Stack books.
  2. Lay cardboard on top.
  3. Pick a car.
  4. Roll and change the ramp.

Parent check: Keep the ramp low and away from stairs.

ramp experiments, flexible building

Needpaper plate, car, books

How to choose safely

Match the activity to the child in front of you, not just the age label. Use larger pieces when younger siblings are nearby, keep ramps and towers low, and supervise tape, pom poms, small blocks, water, rubber bands, or anything that can go in a mouth.
