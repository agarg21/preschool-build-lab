Early math

Math activities for 4 year olds at home.

At age 4, math works best when it is built into play. Counting cups, repeating patterns, comparing distance, and balancing objects all give a child math language without a worksheet.

Quick pick: Use words like more, fewer, longer, shorter, heavier, lighter, same, different, next, and again.

Pattern Path

patterns, sequencing, early math

4-6 years 4 min low mess low help
Needblocks, tiles, or paper squares
  1. Pick two colors.
  2. Make a path.
  3. Say the pattern.
  4. Add one more.

Parent check: Use pieces too large to mouth if younger siblings are nearby.

Measuring Tape Jumps

measurement, comparing distance, active math

4-6 years 5 min low mess medium help
Needpainter tape, toy, floor
  1. Make tape marks.
  2. Jump a toy.
  3. See where it lands.
  4. Try again.

Parent check: Use a toy jump, not kid jumping, in small rooms.

Balance Basket Scale

weight, comparison, early math language

4-6 years 7 min low mess medium help
Needhanger, two baskets, safe objects
  1. Add one object.
  2. Add another side.
  3. Watch it tilt.
  4. Try to balance.

Parent check: Adult sets up the hanger and supervises the scale.

How to choose safely

Match the activity to the child in front of you, not just the age label. Use larger pieces when younger siblings are nearby, keep ramps and towers low, and supervise tape, pom poms, small blocks, water, rubber bands, or anything that can go in a mouth.
