Fine motor

Fine motor activities for preschoolers.

Fine motor practice does not have to look like worksheets. Preschoolers build hand strength and control while dropping, stacking, peeling, scooping, connecting, and balancing.

Quick pick: Use large pieces first. If an item can fit in a mouth, treat it as a direct-supervision activity.

Paper Roll Drop

fine motor practice, repetition, color naming

3-5 years 2 min low mess medium help
Needpaper roll, pom poms
  1. Tape or hold a tube.
  2. Drop a pom pom.
  3. Catch it.
  4. Try another color.

Parent check: Use large items if younger siblings are nearby.

Pom Pom Drop

hand control, sorting, quiet play

3-5 years 3 min low mess medium help
Needcardboard tube, pom poms
  1. Set up a tube.
  2. Drop pom poms.
  3. Catch them.
  4. Sort by color.

How to choose safely

Match the activity to the child in front of you, not just the age label. Use larger pieces when younger siblings are nearby, keep ramps and towers low, and supervise tape, pom poms, small blocks, water, rubber bands, or anything that can go in a mouth.
