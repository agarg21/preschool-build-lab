Age 4 STEM

STEM activities for 4 year olds.

Four year olds learn STEM best when the question is visible: Will it roll farther? Will it float? Will the bridge hold? These activities keep the setup small and give the child something real to test.

Quick pick: Use one question per activity: What do you think will happen, and what changed when we tried again?

Ramp Detective car test

ramps, prediction, measuring

4-6 years 5 min low mess medium help
Needlow ramp, towel or placemat, toy car
  1. Roll on a plain ramp.
  2. Add one road surface.
  3. Guess what will happen.
  4. Mark where the car stops.

Parent check: Keep the rolling area clear.

Tiny Boat Cargo Test

buoyancy, water play, redesign

4-6 years 8 min medium mess medium help
Needfoil, shallow water tray, large cargo pieces
  1. Shape a foil boat.
  2. Float it.
  3. Add one large passenger.
  4. Pinch the sides higher.

Parent check: Use one inch of water, large cargo only, and stay next to the tray.

Magnet Hunt

sorting, prediction, material testing

4-6 years 5 min low mess medium help
Needlarge magnet, safe objects
  1. Pick an object.
  2. Guess if it sticks.
  3. Try the magnet.
  4. Make two piles.

Parent check: Use one large magnet and avoid tiny magnets or batteries.

How to choose safely

Match the activity to the child in front of you, not just the age label. Use larger pieces when younger siblings are nearby, keep ramps and towers low, and supervise tape, pom poms, small blocks, water, rubber bands, or anything that can go in a mouth.
