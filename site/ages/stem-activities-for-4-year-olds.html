Age 4 STEM

STEM activities for 4 year olds.

Four year olds learn STEM best when the question is visible: Will it roll farther? Will it float? Will the bridge hold? These activities keep the setup small and give the child something real to test.

Quick pick: Use one question per activity: What do you think will happen, and what changed when we tried again?

Car Ramp Distance Test

early STEM, measuring, prediction

4-6 years 5 min low mess medium help
Needramp, tape, toy cars
  1. Roll a car.
  2. Mark where it stops.
  3. Try another car.
  4. Compare the marks.

Parent check: Keep the rolling area clear.

Paper Bridge

engineering, problem solving, testing

4-6 years 3 min low mess medium help
Needpaper, two books, toy car
  1. Put books apart.
  2. Lay paper across.
  3. Try a car.
  4. Fold paper stronger.

Parent check: Keep books low and stable.

Sink or Float Tray

prediction, sorting, early science talk

4-6 years 5 min medium mess medium help
Needshallow water tray, safe household objects
  1. Pick one object.
  2. Guess sink or float.
  3. Test it.
  4. Sort and try another.

Parent check: Use a shallow tray and stay next to water.

Magnet Hunt

sorting, prediction, material testing

4-6 years 5 min low mess medium help
Needlarge magnet, safe objects
  1. Pick an object.
  2. Guess if it sticks.
  3. Try the magnet.
  4. Make two piles.

Parent check: Use one large magnet and avoid tiny magnets or batteries.

Shadow Shape Match

light, shapes, cause and effect

4-6 years 5 min low mess medium help
Needflashlight, blocks, wall
  1. Pick a block.
  2. Shine the light.
  3. Find the shadow.
  4. Move it closer.

Pattern Path

patterns, sequencing, early math

4-6 years 4 min low mess low help
Needblocks, tiles, or paper squares
  1. Pick two colors.
  2. Make a path.
  3. Say the pattern.
  4. Add one more.

Parent check: Use pieces too large to mouth if younger siblings are nearby.

Wind Tower Test

structure, stability, redesign

4-6 years 6 min low mess medium help
Needblocks, paper fan
  1. Build a tower.
  2. Fan it gently.
  3. See what falls.
  4. Build it wider.

Parent check: Keep towers low and fan gently.

Color Mixing Cups

prediction, color science, careful pouring

4-6 years 6 min medium mess medium help
Needclear cups, water, food coloring
  1. Fill two cups.
  2. Add two colors.
  3. Pour together.
  4. Name the new color.

Parent check: Use tiny drops and protect the table.

How to choose safely

Match the activity to the child in front of you, not just the age label. Use larger pieces when younger siblings are nearby, keep ramps and towers low, and supervise tape, pom poms, small blocks, water, rubber bands, or anything that can go in a mouth.
