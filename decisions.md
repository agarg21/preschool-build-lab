# Decisions And Decision Gates

## Decisions Made

### 2026-06-28: Keep One Domain With Multiple Page Types

Decision: Use `kidactivitylab.com` for original activities, activity cards, SEO collections, age pages, material pages, and a video archive.

Why: One domain can support multiple kids activity page types as long as the promise stays consistent: practical activities parents can run at home.

### 2026-06-28: Original Content Leads

Decision: Original/tested activity content is the primary content asset. Video curation is allowed only as support.

Why: Thin curation is unlikely to build a durable SEO moat. Parent-tested notes, photos, safety observations, easier/harder modes, and kid-facing instructions create more defensible utility.

### 2026-06-28: Age-4 STEM Is The First Original Testing Cluster

Decision: Start original content testing with five age-4 STEM prompts.

Why: The user can directly test activities with a 4-year-old, and STEM has clear search demand around science, engineering, building, and no-prep activities.

### 2026-06-28: Use `AGENTS.md` As The Central Agent Entrypoint

Decision: Future Codex/agent sessions should start from `AGENTS.md`.

Why: The project now has enough docs and generated files that agents need a canonical orientation file to avoid reading stale strategy or making thin pages.

### 2026-06-20: Start With Free Research

Decision: Do not buy paid SEO tools yet.

Why: The project is still choosing between niches. Free/manual research is enough to eliminate weak options and identify the top 2 for SERP validation.

### 2026-06-20: Use Qualitative Scores

Decision: Treat Phase 1 scoring as directional, not definitive.

Why: No paid-tool volumes or keyword difficulty data have been imported. Exact volume claims would be fake precision.

### 2026-06-20: Avoid AI Chat App As MVP

Decision: Bias toward static content libraries and simple utilities.

Why: The user's constraint says not to recommend an AI chat app first, and SEO needs indexable pages with clear intent matching.

### 2026-06-20: Current Top 2 For Validation

Decision: Provisional top 2 are:

1. Building with kids
2. Jersey City / NYC kids activities

Why: Building content has strong firsthand testing potential and repeatable page templates. Local kids activities has stronger local-intent SEO potential but higher freshness requirements.

### 2026-06-20: Phase 2 Validates Both Top Niches

Decision: Keep both building-with-kids and JC/NYC kids activities alive after SERP validation.

Why: The reviewed SERPs showed small blogs, local publishers, forums, Reddit, venue pages, and older/thin results ranking across both niches.

### 2026-06-20: Publish-Test Building With Kids First

Decision: Recommend building-with-kids as the first publish test.

Why: It has a slightly better launch profile for a solo parent-builder: faster testing, easier original photos, lower freshness burden, and a repeatable content engine.

JC/NYC kids activities remains a strong alternate if the user wants a local project and is willing to maintain logistics.

## Continue Gates

Continue a niche to the next phase if:

- Overall score is 75+.
- At least 40 long-tail queries have clear parent intent.
- At least 15 queries look like realistic ranking opportunities after manual SERP review.
- Content can be improved through firsthand parent testing, photos, checklists, maps, or age-fit notes.
- Safety concerns can be handled with practical disclaimers and conservative recommendations.

## Pause Gates

Pause a niche if:

- It scores 65-74 and needs narrower positioning.
- It depends on seasonal or local freshness the user cannot maintain.
- It needs downloadable assets, photography, or structured data that cannot be produced yet.
- SERPs are mixed-intent and unclear.

## Kill Gates

Kill a niche if:

- It scores below 65 after a fair Phase 1 pass.
- Top SERPs are overwhelmingly dominated by major domains with no weak spots.
- The content requires medical, therapeutic, educational, or safety claims beyond the user's ability to verify.
- There is no clear way to produce 20-30 genuinely useful pages.
- The distribution channels are weak or unauthentic.

## Paid Tool Gates

Use LowFruits or KeySearch if:

- Two niches score above 75.
- Manual SERP review finds many plausible weak-result queries.
- We need batch validation before choosing.
- The first 10 article briefs are accepted and we want to batch-check the next 100 keywords.

Use Ahrefs or Semrush for one month if:

- One niche is clearly selected.
- We are ready to publish 20-30 pages.
- We need competitor top-page data, content gaps, backlinks, and adjacent keyword clusters.

Use Screaming Frog later if:

- A site has launched.
- There are more than 50-100 URLs or technical SEO issues to inspect.
- Free 500-URL crawl is likely enough at first.
